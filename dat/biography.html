I am an aspiring professor and a Ph.D. candidate at UC Berkeley working in the RISE Lab. I research experimentation in MLOps. Specifically, processes and tools for ML engineers to iterate on models and data with high velocity and low friction. This work spans topics in data systems, software engineering, and applied ML. I'm the primary developer and maintainer of flor, a record-replay system for hindsight logging & ML training (in Python). We wrote all about it in our latest paper Hindsight Logging for Model Training. I am advised by Joe Hellerstein, and am a fortunate recipient of UC Berkeley Chancellor's Fellowship and the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship.
