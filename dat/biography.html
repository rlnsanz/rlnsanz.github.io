I am a Presidential Postdoctoral Fellow at Arizona State University's School of Computing and Augmented Intelligence. I completed my PhD in computer science at UC Berkeley, where I studied the management of context (or metadata) in the machine learning lifecycle. Advised by Joe Hellerstein, my research unifies themes from databases, software engineering, and applied ML.

I am the chief developer and maintainer of FlorDB, a hindsight logging database for the machine learning lifecycle (GitHub, YouTube). My PhD was funded by a UC Berkeley Chancellor's Fellowship and an NSF Graduate Research Fellowship, further supported by a generous grant from Dell Inc. You can learn more about my academic journey and contributions by viewing my CV and Scholar. I'll also be posting more on LinkedIn.
