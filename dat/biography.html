I am a Ph.D. candidate at UC Berkeley working in the EPIC Data Lab (formerly RISE Lab). I research experimentation in MLOps. Specifically, processes and tools for ML engineers to iterate on models and data in production, or people-facing, environments. This work spans topics in data systems, software engineering, and applied ML. I'm the primary developer and maintainer of flor, a record-replay system for hindsight logging & ML training (in Python). We wrote all about it in our latest paper Hindsight Logging for Model Training. I am advised by Joe Hellerstein, and am a fortunate recipient of UC Berkeley Chancellor's Fellowship and the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship.
