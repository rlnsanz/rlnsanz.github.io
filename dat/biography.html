A Ph.D. candidate at UC Berkeley and aspiring professor, I have the privilege of conducting research at the RISE Lab. My focus centers on experimentation in MLOps, specifically exploring processes and tools that enable ML engineers to refine models and data within production or human-centric environments. This extensive work unifies themes from data systems, software engineering, and applied ML. I am the chief developer and maintainer of FlorFlow, a context-driven platform for streamlining AI/ML development and experimentation. Guided and mentored by Joe Hellerstein, I have been honored to receive the UC Berkeley Chancellor's Fellowship and the NSF Graduate Research Fellowship, further supported by a generous grant from Dell Inc. You can learn more about my academic journey and contributions by viewing my CV. Additionally, I occasionally share thoughts and insights via Mastodon.
